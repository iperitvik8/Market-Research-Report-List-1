<p><p>The Pediatric Lower Limb Orthoses market is divided into two primary types: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to allow movement while providing support, promoting functional mobility and independence in children with various conditions. In contrast, static orthotics are rigid and aim to immobilize specific joints or areas, providing stability and alignment for conditions like cerebral palsy or developmental delays. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing mobility and overall quality of life in pediatric patients.</p></p>
