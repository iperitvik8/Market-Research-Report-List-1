<p><p>The IR emitter and receiver market is categorized based on wavelength ranges: short wavelength infrared (SWIR) covers 0.9 to 1.7 micrometers, used in applications like spectroscopy and surveillance; medium wavelength infrared (MWIR), ranging from 3 to 5 micrometers, is vital for thermal imaging and industrial monitoring; long wavelength infrared (LWIR), spanning 8 to 12 micrometers, serves in night vision and environmental sensing; and far infrared (FIR), beyond 15 micrometers, primarily finds use in heating applications and thermal therapy.</p></p>
